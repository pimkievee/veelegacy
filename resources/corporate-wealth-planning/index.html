Corporate Wealth Planning for Business Owners

Business owners often need planning that connects retained earnings, personal income, corporate assets, protection, and succession priorities.

What is corporate wealth planning?

Corporate wealth planning looks at how a business owner uses the corporation, personal accounts, insurance, investments, and succession strategy as one coordinated financial picture.

Planning themes

  • Retained earnings and cash flow needs
  • Corporate investment strategy and risk profile
  • Key person and shareholder protection
  • Corporate-owned life insurance conversations
  • Succession, continuity, and estate coordination

Why business owners need coordination

Decisions inside the corporation can affect personal income, taxes, retirement, estate plans, and family wealth. A structured review helps avoid disconnected choices.

Corporate planning often links with life insurance, estate planning, and retirement planning.
